On this page line intersection is tested. There's one fixed line, and you can input the parameters of the other line manually.

Line intersection is calculated by solving a system of equations containing both lines' equations. The result is the x and y coordinates of the point where the lines intersect.

Most often two lines have one point in common. There are two special cases: if the lines are parallel, they have no common points, and if the lines are equal, they have an infinite number of points in common.
